How to play Penalty Shootout

It's you against the computer in a classic football penalty shootout. Each side takes five penalty kicks, and whoever scores more wins. You play both roles every round: first you take a kick, then you try to save the computer's kick.

When you're shooting, choose a corner — left, centre, or right. The goalkeeper commits to a direction at the same moment; if they guess wrong, the ball hits the net. When you're saving, pick which way to dive — guess the same direction the striker shoots and you'll keep it out. After five kicks each, if the score is level, it goes to sudden death: one kick each per round until someone comes out ahead.

Tips to win the shootout

  • Mix up your corners. A smart keeper will read a pattern, so don't keep aiming the same way.
  • The centre is sneaky. Keepers often dive to a side, so straight down the middle can pay off.
  • When saving, watch the rhythm. On harder levels the computer is a better shooter — trust your read and commit.
  • Stay cool in sudden death. One save or one goal can settle it; don't get predictable when it matters most.

Frequently asked questions

Is Penalty Shootout free?

Yes — it plays free in your browser with no ads, no sign-in, and nothing to install.

Do I shoot, save, or both?

Both. Each round you take a penalty and then try to save the computer's penalty.

What happens if it's a tie?

After five kicks each, a level score goes to sudden death — one kick each per round until someone leads.

Is this an official World Cup or FIFA game?

No. It's an original SnackPack game with no affiliation to any official tournament or governing body — just a fun penalty shootout to play.
