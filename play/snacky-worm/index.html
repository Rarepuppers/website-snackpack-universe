How to play Snacky Worm

Snacky Worm is the timeless Snake game. You control a worm that's always on the move, and your job is to guide its head onto the food. Each bite makes the worm one segment longer and adds a point. The catch: the longer you get, the more of the board your own body fills — and running into yourself (or a wall) ends the game. Steer with the arrow keys or WASD on a keyboard, swipe on the board, or use the on-screen pad. You can't turn back on yourself in a single step, so plan your turns a moment ahead.

Modes

  • Classic — standard speed; the walls are deadly.
  • Zen — same speed, but the worm wraps around the edges instead of crashing, so the only threat is yourself.
  • Speed — a faster worm for a tougher challenge and quicker rounds.

Tips for a longer worm

  • Use the whole board early. When you're short, grab food efficiently and keep the middle clear for later.
  • Follow the walls when you're long. Hugging the edges leaves open space in the centre to manoeuvre.
  • Leave yourself an exit. Before you dive into a gap for food, check you can get back out.
  • Turn early, not late. Because you can't reverse, commit to turns with room to spare.
  • Try Zen to practise. Wrapping walls let you focus purely on not crossing your own tail.

About Snacky Worm

Snake games date back to the 1976 arcade game Blockade, but the genre became a household name when Nokia loaded Snake onto its mobile phones in 1998 — quietly one of the most-played video games of all time. Snacky Worm is SnackPack Studios' calm, ad-free version: no interruptions, no tracking, just one more go. Frequently asked questions

Is Snacky Worm free?

Yes — it plays free in your browser with no ads, no sign-in, and nothing to install.

What are the controls?

Arrow keys or WASD on a keyboard, swipe on the board, or the on-screen direction pad.

What does Zen mode do?

In Zen mode the worm wraps around the edges of the board instead of crashing into the walls, so the only way to lose is to run into yourself.
