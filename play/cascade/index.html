How to play Cascade

Cascade is a falling-blocks puzzle played in a tall well, ten columns wide and twenty rows high. Shapes made of four blocks — the seven classic tetromino forms — fall one at a time from the top. Move each shape left and right and rotate it so the blocks fit neatly together. When you completely fill a horizontal row, it clears and you score; clear several rows at once for a much bigger bonus. The game ends when a new shape can no longer enter the well, so the goal is simply to last as long as you can and build the highest score.

On a computer, use the arrow keys to move and soft-drop, the up arrow (or Z and X) to rotate, and the space bar to hard-drop a piece instantly. On a touchscreen, use the buttons below the board, and tap the left or right half of the well to rotate. Clearing four rows in a single drop — a full Cascade — is the big score, and the speed ramps up as you clear more lines.

Strategy tips

  • Keep the stack flat. An even surface gives you the most places to drop the next piece.
  • Leave a well for the long piece. Keeping one column open lets you clear several rows at once for a big bonus.
  • Use the Next preview. Plan your current move around the shape that's coming.
  • Rotate near the floor. Last-moment rotations can slot a piece into an awkward gap.
  • Don't bury holes. A covered empty cell is hard to recover — clear above it as soon as you can.

About Cascade

Cascade is SnackPack Studios' calm, ad-free take on the falling-blocks genre that began with Alexey Pajitnov's Tetris in 1984 — one of the most influential video games ever made. Like the rest of the SnackPack lineup, it's built to be a relaxing few minutes rather than a noisy grind: no ads, no tracking, just the puzzle. The full version, with a Turbo start mode and saved top scores, is part of the Snackpack Brain Games app.

Frequently asked questions

Is Cascade free?

Yes — it plays free in your browser with no ads, no sign-in, and nothing to install.

What are the controls?

Arrow keys to move and soft-drop, up arrow or Z/X to rotate, space to hard-drop, P to pause — or the on-screen buttons and board taps on touch devices.

How does scoring work?

Clearing one to four rows scores 100, 300, 500 or 800 points, multiplied by your level. Soft and hard drops add small bonuses.

Can I play offline?

The web version needs an open browser tab. For fully offline play with a Turbo mode and saved top scores, install the free Snackpack Brain Games app on Android.
